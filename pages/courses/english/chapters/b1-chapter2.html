English B1 - Chapter 2: Making Suggestions

B1 - Threshold

Making Suggestions

At the B1 level, you can communicate with some confidence on familiar routine and non-routine matters related to your interests and professional field. You can suggest courses of action, make recommendations, and express ideas.

Common Expressions for Making Suggestions

  • Let's... - "Let's go to the cinema tonight."
  • How about...? - "How about trying that new restaurant?"
  • Why don't we/you...? - "Why don't we meet earlier?"
  • We could... - "We could visit your parents this weekend."
  • What about...? - "What about going for a walk?"
  • I suggest that we... - "I suggest that we postpone the meeting."
  • Have you thought about...? - "Have you thought about joining a gym?"
  • It might be a good idea to... - "It might be a good idea to book in advance."
  • If I were you, I'd... - "If I were you, I'd speak to the manager."

Responding to Suggestions

  • Accepting: "That sounds good.", "Great idea!", "Why not?", "I'm up for that."
  • Rejecting politely: "I'd rather not.", "I'm not sure about that.", "I don't think that would work because...", "Maybe another time."
