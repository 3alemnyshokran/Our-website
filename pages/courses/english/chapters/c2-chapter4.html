C2 Chapter 4: Advanced Pragmatics and Sociolinguistics

At the C2 level, you can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read. You can summarize information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation. You can express yourself spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations.

Pragmatic Competence

C2 level users demonstrate sophisticated pragmatic awareness:

  • Implied meaning and presupposition: Understanding what is meant but not explicitly stated
  • Speech acts: Mastering indirect requests, subtle complaints, delicate refusals
  • Cooperative principle: Knowing when and how to flout conversational maxims for effect
  • Politeness strategies: Using elaborate negative politeness ("I was wondering if you might possibly consider...")

Sociolinguistic Awareness

At C2 level, you should be able to:

  • Switch between dialects, accents, and regional varieties
  • Understand and use slang, colloquialisms, and jargon appropriately
  • Recognize language change and contemporary usage trends
  • Navigate sensitive topics with appropriate language choices

Practice Questions

1. What is the implied meaning in this exchange?
A: "Are you going to the meeting?"
B: "Well, I've got a deadline to meet."

Answer: B is indirectly saying no, implying that the deadline takes priority and prevents attendance at the meeting.

2. Rewrite this direct request as a more sophisticated indirect request suitable for a formal context: "Give me the report by Friday."

Answer: I was wondering if it might be possible for you to submit the report by Friday, if that wouldn't be too inconvenient.

3. How would you explain the difference between these two sentences in terms of register?
a) "The committee has reached a decision on the matter."
b) "They've figured out what to do about that thing."

Answer: Sentence (a) uses formal register with precise vocabulary ("committee," "reached a decision," "matter"), while sentence (b) uses informal register with casual phrasing ("figured out," "that thing") and contractions.
