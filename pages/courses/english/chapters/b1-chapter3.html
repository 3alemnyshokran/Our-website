English B1 - Chapter 3: Expressing Opinions

B1 - Threshold

Expressing Opinions

At the B1 level, you can express your thoughts, feelings, and opinions on a range of topics with some confidence. You can engage in discussions and give reasons for your views.

Common Expressions for Stating Opinions

  • I think/believe that... - "I think that social media has both positive and negative effects."
  • In my opinion... - "In my opinion, the government should invest more in education."
  • From my point of view... - "From my point of view, environmental protection is essential."
  • It seems to me that... - "It seems to me that the solution is more complex than it appears."
  • As far as I'm concerned... - "As far as I'm concerned, everyone should have access to healthcare."
  • I feel that... - "I feel that more research is needed on this topic."
  • I'd say that... - "I'd say that the book is worth reading despite its flaws."
  • To my mind... - "To my mind, the advantages outweigh the disadvantages."

Expressing Agreement and Disagreement

  • Agreeing: "I completely agree.", "That's exactly what I think.", "You're absolutely right.", "I couldn't agree more."
  • Partly agreeing: "I agree to some extent, but...", "I see your point, however...", "That's true, although..."
  • Disagreeing politely: "I'm afraid I don't agree.", "I see it differently.", "I respect your opinion, but I think...", "I'm not sure I agree with that."

Giving Reasons for Opinions

  • Supporting with facts: "According to recent studies...", "The statistics show that...", "Research indicates that..."
  • Giving examples: "For instance...", "For example...", "To illustrate this point..."
  • Using personal experience: "In my experience...", "From what I've seen...", "Based on my own observations..."
