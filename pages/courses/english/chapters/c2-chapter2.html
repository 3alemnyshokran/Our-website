C2 Chapter 2: Academic and Professional Discourse

At the C2 level, you can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read. You can summarize information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation. You can express yourself spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations.

Academic Writing Conventions

C2 level users can navigate complex academic conventions:

  • Hedging and cautious language: "Research suggests that...", "It appears that...", "The evidence tends to indicate..."
  • Critical evaluation: "While X argues convincingly that..., the evidence presented fails to account for..."
  • Cohesion and coherence markers: "Notwithstanding the aforementioned limitations...", "In light of these considerations..."
  • Objective stance and impersonal structures: "It can be observed that..." instead of "I can see that..."

Professional and Technical Language

At C2 level, you should be able to:

  • Use field-specific terminology with precision
  • Employ nominalization for formal effect: "The implementation of the strategy" instead of "implementing the strategy"
  • Structure complex arguments with subordination: "The proposal, which was initially rejected by the board on the grounds that it lacked sufficient evidence of feasibility, has subsequently been approved following a comprehensive review."

Practice Questions

1. Rewrite this sentence using appropriate academic language: "I think this theory is wrong because it doesn't match the facts."

Answer: This theory appears to be inconsistent with the empirical evidence, suggesting that its fundamental premises may require reconsideration.

2. Convert this informal sentence to a more formal, professional one: "We looked at the sales figures and found they went down last quarter."

Answer: An analysis of the quarterly sales figures revealed a downward trend in the previous period.

3. Identify the academic writing conventions used in this sentence: "While the research methodology appears robust, the sample size raises questions about the generalizability of the findings."

Answer: Hedging language ("appears robust"), critical evaluation, academic vocabulary ("methodology," "generalizability")
