Shopping

At the A2 level, you can communicate in simple and routine tasks requiring a simple and direct exchange of information on familiar matters, such as shopping.

Types of Shops

Common types of shops and what you can buy there:

  • Supermarket: food, drinks, household items
  • Bakery: bread, cakes, pastries
  • Butcher's: meat
  • Greengrocer's: fruits and vegetables
  • Pharmacy/Chemist's: medicine, toiletries
  • Clothing store: clothes, shoes, accessories
  • Bookshop: books, magazines, stationery
  • Electronics store: computers, phones, gadgets

Shopping Phrases and Dialogues

Useful phrases for shopping:

  • Asking for help: "Excuse me, can you help me?", "I'm looking for..."
  • Asking about price: "How much is this?", "How much does it cost?"
  • Trying clothes: "Can I try this on?", "Where are the fitting rooms?"
  • Paying: "Can I pay by card/cash?", "Do you accept credit cards?"
  • Other phrases: "Do you have this in a different size/color?", "I'll take it."

Sample Dialogue

Customer: Excuse me, I'm looking for a blue shirt.
Shop assistant: Yes, the shirts are over there. What size do you need?
Customer: Medium, please.
Shop assistant: Here you are. The medium blue shirts.
Customer: Thank you. How much is this one?
Shop assistant: That's $25.
Customer: Can I try it on?
Shop assistant: Yes, the fitting rooms are over there.
Customer: I'll take it. Can I pay by card?
Shop assistant: Yes, of course.
