Simple Present Tense - What is it?

The simple present tense is used to talk about:

زمن المضارع البسيط يستخدم للحديث عن:

  • Habits and routines: "I wake up at 7 AM every day."
  • Facts and general truths: "The sun rises in the east."
  • Fixed arrangements: "The train leaves at 9 PM."
  • Instructions and directions: "First, you mix the flour and butter."
  • العادات والروتين: "أستيقظ الساعة 7 صباحًا كل يوم."
  • الحقائق والقوانين العامة: "تشرق الشمس من الشرق."
  • الترتيبات الثابتة: "يغادر القطار الساعة 9 مساءً."
  • التعليمات والتوجيهات: "أولًا، تخلط الدقيق والزبدة."

Form of Simple Present

For most verbs: Subject + Base Form (infinitive without "to")

For 3rd person singular (he/she/it): Subject + Base Form + s/es

صيغة المضارع البسيط:
لمعظم الأفعال: الفاعل + الفعل الأساسي (المصدر بدون "to")
للشخص الثالث المفرد (هو/هي/هو): الفاعل + الفعل الأساسي + s/es

Subject Positive Negative Question
I I work I do not work
(I don't work)		 Do I work?
You You work You do not work
(You don't work)		 Do you work?
He/She/It He works He does not work
(He doesn't work)		 Does he work?
We We work We do not work
(We don't work)		 Do we work?
They They work They do not work
(They don't work)		 Do they work?

Special Rules for Third Person Singular (he/she/it)

When using the simple present tense with he, she, or it, we add -s or -es to the base form of the verb:

Adding -s:

Most verbs: play → plays, eat → eats, run → runs

Adding -es:

Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -z, -o: watch → watches, wash → washes, kiss → kisses, fix → fixes, buzz → buzzes, go → goes

Verbs ending in consonant + y:

Change y to i and add -es: study → studies, try → tries

But if y follows a vowel, just add -s: play → plays, say → says

Using Simple Present for Daily Routines

The simple present tense is commonly used to talk about daily routines and habits:

  • "I wake up at 6:30 AM."
  • "He takes a shower every morning."
  • "We have breakfast at 8:00 AM."
  • "They go to work by bus."
  • "She studies after dinner."

We often use time expressions with the simple present tense:

  • Every day/week/month/year
  • On Mondays/Tuesdays/etc.
  • Always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
  • In the morning/afternoon/evening

Negative Statements and Questions

To form negative statements in the simple present tense, we use "do not" (don't) or "does not" (doesn't):

لتكوين جمل منفية في زمن المضارع البسيط، نستخدم "do not" (don't) أو "does not" (doesn't):

I/You/We/They + do not (don't) + base form

He/She/It + does not (doesn't) + base form

أنا/أنت/نحن/هم + do not (don't) + الفعل الأساسي
هو/هي + does not (doesn't) + الفعل الأساسي

Examples:

  • "I don't like coffee."
  • "She doesn't speak French."

أمثلة:
"أنا لا أحب القهوة."
"هي لا تتحدث الفرنسية."

To form questions in the simple present tense, we use "do" or "does":

لتكوين أسئلة في زمن المضارع البسيط، نستخدم "do" أو "does":

Do + I/you/we/they + base form?

Does + he/she/it + base form?

Do + أنا/أنت/نحن/هم + الفعل الأساسي؟
Does + هو/هي + الفعل الأساسي؟

Examples:

  • "Do you play soccer?"
  • "Does he live in London?"

أمثلة:
"هل تلعب كرة القدم؟"
"هل يعيش في لندن؟"

English Levels (CEFR): A1 to C2

A1 – Beginner

  • Understands and uses everyday expressions and basic phrases.
  • Can introduce themselves and answer simple questions.
  • Needs others to speak slowly and clearly.

المستوى A1 - المبتدئ
- يفهم ويستخدم التعبيرات اليومية والعبارات الأساسية.
- يمكنه تقديم نفسه والإجابة على الأسئلة البسيطة.
- يحتاج الآخرين للتحدث ببطء ووضوح.

A2 – Elementary

  • Can communicate in simple, routine tasks.
  • Understands frequently used expressions related to areas of immediate relevance.
  • Can describe in simple terms aspects of their background and immediate environment.

B1 – Intermediate

  • Can deal with most situations likely to arise while traveling.
  • Can produce simple connected text on familiar topics.
  • Can describe experiences, events, dreams, and ambitions, and briefly give reasons and explanations.

B2 – Upper-Intermediate

  • Can interact with native speakers with a degree of fluency and spontaneity.
  • Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects.
  • Can explain a viewpoint on a topical issue.

C1 – Advanced

  • Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes.
  • Can produce well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects.
  • Understands a wide range of demanding, longer texts.

C2 – Mastery

  • Can understand virtually everything heard or read with ease.
  • Can summarize information from different spoken and written sources.
  • Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely, even in complex situations.

Why CEFR Levels Matter

CEFR levels help learners, teachers, and employers understand language ability. They are used worldwide in courses, exams, and job applications.

How to Progress

  • Practice reading, writing, listening, and speaking regularly.
  • Use structured lessons, instant feedback, and real-life practice.
  • Move step by step: build a strong foundation at A1, then progress to higher levels.
