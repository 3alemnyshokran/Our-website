Common Greetings

Learning how to greet people is one of the first steps in any language. Here are some common English greetings:

Hello / Hi
/həˈləʊ/ / /haɪ/
Used as a general greeting at any time of day.
"Hello, nice to meet you." / "Hi there!"
Good morning
/ɡʊd ˈmɔːnɪŋ/
Used as a greeting before noon.
"Good morning! How are you today?"
Good afternoon
/ɡʊd ɑːftəˈnuːn/
Used as a greeting between noon and evening.
"Good afternoon, everyone!"
Good evening
/ɡʊd ˈiːvnɪŋ/
Used as a greeting in the evening.
"Good evening! Welcome to our restaurant."

Introducing Yourself

Here are some common phrases for introducing yourself:

My name is...
/maɪ neɪm ɪz/
Used to tell someone your name.
"Hello! My name is John."
I am from...
/aɪ æm frɒm/
Used to tell someone where you're from.
"I am from Spain."
Nice to meet you
/naɪs tuː miːt juː/
Used when meeting someone for the first time.
"Hello, I'm Sarah. Nice to meet you!"

Basic Questions and Responses

Here are some common questions and ways to respond:

How are you?
/haʊ ɑː juː/
Used to ask someone how they're feeling.
"How are you?"
"I'm fine, thank you. And you?"
What's your name?
/wɒts jɔː neɪm/
Used to ask someone their name.
"What's your name?"
"My name is Maria."
Where are you from?
/weər ɑː juː frɒm/
Used to ask someone about their country or city of origin.
"Where are you from?"
"I'm from Brazil."

Saying Goodbye

Here are some common ways to say goodbye in English:

Goodbye / Bye
/ɡʊdˈbaɪ/ / /baɪ/
Used when leaving or ending a conversation.
"Goodbye, see you tomorrow." / "Bye!"
See you later / See you soon
/siː juː ˈleɪtə/ / /siː juː suːn/
Used when you expect to meet the person again relatively soon.
"I have to go now. See you later!"
Good night
/ɡʊd naɪt/
Used when saying goodbye in the evening or before sleeping.
"Good night, sleep well."
