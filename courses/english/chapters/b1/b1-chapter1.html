Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect is used for experiences and actions that have happened at an unspecified time. Example: "I have visited London."

Subject Have/Has Past Participle Example
I have worked I have worked here for two years.
You have seen You have seen this movie before.
He/She/It has been She has been to Paris twice.
We have eaten We have eaten Italian food before.
They have lived They have lived here since 2010.

Time Expressions with Present Perfect

Several time expressions are commonly used with the present perfect tense:

  • Ever - "Have you ever been to Japan?" (asking about experiences)
  • Never - "I have never tried sushi." (negative experience)
  • Just - "I have just finished my homework." (very recent past)
  • Already - "She has already seen that movie." (something happened earlier than expected)
  • Yet - "Have you done your homework yet?" (up to now, in questions and negatives)
  • Since - "I have lived here since 2010." (from a specific point in the past until now)
  • For - "She has worked here for five years." (duration)

Expressing Opinions

At the B1 level, you can express more complex opinions using a variety of phrases:

  • "In my opinion, this restaurant is excellent."
  • "I believe that learning languages is important."
  • "I think that the government should invest more in education."
  • "From my point of view, the solution is simple."
  • "I agree/disagree with the idea that..."
