Grammar: Narrative Tenses

Past Simple

Used for completed actions in the past, often the main events in a story.

  • Form: Subject + V2 (past form)
  • Example: "I arrived at the party at 8 PM."

Past Continuous

Used for actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past or for setting the scene.

  • Form: Subject + was/were + verb-ing
  • Example: "When I arrived, they were dancing in the living room."

Past Perfect

Used for actions that happened before another action in the past.

  • Form: Subject + had + past participle
  • Example: "By the time I arrived, most guests had already left."

Past Perfect Continuous

Used for ongoing actions that started before and continued up to another action in the past.

  • Form: Subject + had been + verb-ing
  • Example: "She was tired because she had been working all night."

Using Narrative Tenses Together

Example paragraph:

"It was raining (past continuous - setting) when I arrived (past simple - main event) at the station. The train had already left (past perfect - earlier event) by the time I got there. I had been waiting (past perfect continuous - ongoing action) for a taxi for twenty minutes when my friend called (past simple - main event) to offer me a ride."

Practice Exercise 1: Identifying Narrative Tenses

Read the following story and identify the different narrative tenses used:

Last summer, I visited my grandparents who lived in a small coastal town. When I arrived, my grandmother was cooking dinner. She had prepared my favorite dessert earlier that day. My grandfather had been working in the garden all afternoon and was quite tired. After dinner, we sat on the porch and watched the sunset. It was the most beautiful sunset I had ever seen.

  • Past simple: visited, lived, arrived, sat, watched, was
  • Past continuous: was cooking
  • Past perfect: had prepared, had ever seen
  • Past perfect continuous: had been working

Practice Exercise 2: Complete the Story

Fill in the gaps with the correct form of the verbs in brackets, using appropriate narrative tenses:

  1. By the time we reached the theater, the movie ____________ (already/start).
  2. Sarah ____________ (walk) down the street when she ____________ (notice) the old bookshop.
  3. They ____________ (wait) for three hours before the bus finally ____________ (arrive).
  4. When I ____________ (get) to the restaurant, my friends ____________ (already/order) food.
  5. The ground was wet because it ____________ (rain) all night.
  6. I ____________ (study) English for five years before I ____________ (move) to London.
  7. While he ____________ (sleep), someone ____________ (break) into his house.
  8. She ____________ (just/finish) her homework when her friends ____________ (call).
  1. had already started
  2. was walking, noticed
  3. had been waiting, arrived
  4. got, had already ordered
  5. had been raining
  6. had been studying, moved
  7. was sleeping, broke
  8. had just finished, called

Speaking Activity: Tell a Story

Prepare a short story (2-3 minutes) about an interesting, surprising, or frightening experience you have had. Use the following structure:

  • Setting the scene: Use past continuous to describe where you were and what was happening
  • Background information: Use past perfect to mention events that had happened before
  • Main events: Use past simple to tell what happened
  • Duration: Use past perfect continuous for actions that had been happening for some time
  • Use time expressions to connect different parts of your story

Remember to include how you felt and what you learned from the experience.
