Expressing Opinions Practice

Test your ability to express and understand opinions in English.

0/10 Questions

Question 1: Expressing Preferences

Which phrase is best for expressing a preference in a polite way?

I want this one.
You should choose this one.
I'd prefer this one, if that's okay.
This is the best one.

Question 2: Agreeing with an Opinion

Someone says: "I think the film was really interesting." Which response shows that you agree?

I don't think so.
You're absolutely right. I enjoyed it too.
That's just your opinion.
I see what you mean, but I found it boring.

Question 3: Disagreeing Politely

Which is the most polite way to disagree with someone's opinion?

You're wrong about that.
I couldn't disagree more.
That's a terrible idea.
I see your point, but I have a different perspective.

Question 4: Asking for Opinions

Which question is best for asking someone's opinion about a serious topic?

Do you like this?
What do you think about this issue?
Is this good or bad?
Do you agree with me?

Question 5: Introducing an Opinion

Which phrase is best for introducing your opinion in a formal discussion?

I know that...
Listen to me...
In my opinion...
You should know that...

Question 6: Neutral Language

Which sentence uses neutral language to express an opinion?

This restaurant is definitely the best in town.
Only a fool would eat at that restaurant.
Based on my experience, the service at this restaurant is quite good.
Everyone knows this restaurant is overpriced.

Question 7: Strong Opinions

Which phrase expresses a strong opinion in an acceptable way?

I strongly believe that this approach would be beneficial.
Only an idiot would suggest such a thing.
You must be joking if you think that's a good idea.
Everyone with any sense agrees with me.

Question 8: Partially Agreeing

Which phrase is best for partially agreeing with someone?

Yes, but you're wrong about...
I agree with some parts of what you said, particularly...
You've got it half right.
Let me tell you where you're mistaken...

Question 9: Comparing Opinions

Which sentence correctly compares opinions?

My opinion is better than yours.
You think that, but I think this.
While some people believe X, others argue that Y.
Most experts disagree with you.

Question 10: Opinion vs. Fact

Which statement represents an opinion rather than a fact?

Paris is the capital of France.
The Earth orbits around the Sun.
Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius at sea level.
This painting is the most beautiful artwork in the museum.
