Key Vocabulary for Debates

Learn essential vocabulary for participating in discussions and debates:

  • Debate, discuss, argument, point of view, agree, disagree, suggest, recommend
  • In my opinion, from my perspective, as far as I'm concerned
  • I see your point, however..., That's a valid argument, but...

Modal Verbs for Opinion

Use modal verbs to express opinions and suggestions. Example: "You should try this method."

  • We must consider all the options.
  • You might be right.
  • Could you explain your point of view?
Modal Verb Usage Example
Should Advice/Suggestion You should consider both sides.
Must Strong obligation We must find a solution.
Might/May Possibility That might be true.
Could Possibility/Suggestion We could try a different approach.

Expressing Agreement and Disagreement

Learn how to politely agree or disagree in a debate:

  • Agreement: I completely agree, That's exactly what I think, You're absolutely right
  • Partial Agreement: I agree to some extent, You have a point there, I see what you mean
  • Disagreement: I'm afraid I don't agree, I see it differently, I respectfully disagree
